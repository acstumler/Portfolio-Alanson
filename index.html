About

I’m based in Louisville, Kentucky, and I work with operationally complex firms on a problem most of them know intimately but few have solved: the information that runs their most important work is scattered across disconnected systems, and the judgment that actually drives it lives in the heads of a few experienced people. My background is in accounting and finance, including more than seven years in tax and advisory, which gives me a close view of how these businesses run, and how much organized, reliable data is worth to them.

That used to be a chronic inefficiency. AI is turning it into an urgent one. Firms whose data is organized and ready pull further ahead as the tools improve; the ones still running on fragmented information and memory don’t, and the gap tends to widen quietly. My focus is helping operationally complex businesses close it, turning information that’s hard to use into a foundation they can decide and compete on.

What that adds up to is a better-run operation, one built on organized data and clear judgment instead of guesswork. I’m drawn to the point where strong systems meet clean, intuitive design.
